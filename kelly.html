About Kelly

Kelly works with the full spectrum of sexuality—from recovery from compulsive or compulsively avoidant sexual behaviors to healing from infidelity, rebuilding trust, and revitalizing intimacy. She has helped countless individuals progress through recovery and supported couples in overcoming betrayal while restoring closeness in their relationships.

A graduate of Western Seminary, Kelly has also served as an adjunct professor teaching Case Conference courses to Master's level students and has held adjunct positions at Multnomah University and Concordia University. She is dedicated to continuous growth in her knowledge and clinical skills so you receive thoughtful, current care.

Kelly’s priority is to help you discover the freedom and joy that life has to offer. Online therapy services are available when distance or time make in-person sessions challenging.

Therapy process

  • Consultation: A phone call to confirm we're a good fit.
  • Intake & first session: We review your goals and history together.
  • Ongoing therapy: Sessions continue for as long as you find value—some clients stay for a focused 3 months, others for many years.

How we work together

Sessions are tailored to your pace and needs, with a focus on safety, clarity, and collaborative goals. Kelly blends specialized training in sex therapy, addiction recovery, and betrayal trauma to support both individuals and couples.
